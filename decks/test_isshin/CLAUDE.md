# Isshin, Two Heavens as One — Mardu Attack Triggers (TEST OPPONENT)

## THIS IS A TEST OPPONENT DECK — NOT OWNED
Source: MTGGoldfish Budget Commander ($60)

## Commander
Isshin, Two Heavens as One

## Bracket
3 (Upgraded — no Game Changers, but strong synergy-driven power)

## Theme
Mardu attack triggers doubled by Isshin. When creatures attack, their "whenever this creature attacks" triggers happen twice. Exalted, token generation, equipment buffs, and combat damage all doubled. Voltron-aggro hybrid with enchantment-based pillowfort.

## Strategy
1. T1-2: Deploy mana rocks (Boros/Orzhov/Rakdos Signets) and cheap creatures
2. T3: Cast Isshin. Now every attack trigger is doubled.
3. T4+: Equip and attack. Sublime Archangel exalted doubled = massive single attacker. Mardu Strike Leader makes 2 tokens per attack. Combat Calligrapher gives opponents tokens that can't attack you.

## Mulligan Guide
- KEEP: 3 lands (all 3 colors ideal) + signet + creature with attack trigger
- KEEP: 2 lands + Sol Ring + any 2 spells
- MULLIGAN: Single color in a 3-color deck. No creatures. All equipment with nothing to equip.

## Win Conditions
- Sublime Archangel + Isshin doubling exalted = one creature with +10/+10
- Relentless Assault for extra combat (all triggers fire again)
- Parhelion II crewed = makes 4 angel tokens per attack (doubled by Isshin)
- Etali, Primal Storm casting 2 free spells per attack
- Seraphic Greatsword making angel tokens + equipment damage

## Known Weakness
- Isshin must survive for the strategy to work — 3 toughness is fragile
- Three-color manabase with budget lands = inconsistent
- Needs board presence AND Isshin — one without the other is weak
- Limited card draw — can run out of gas quickly
