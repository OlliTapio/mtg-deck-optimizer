# Xenagos, God of Revels — Gruul Big Creatures (TEST OPPONENT)

## THIS IS A TEST OPPONENT DECK — NOT OWNED
Source: EDHREC Average Decklist

## Commander
Xenagos, God of Revels

## Bracket
3 (Upgraded — 1 Game Changer: Worldly Tutor)

## Theme
Gruul "big stompy" with extra combats. Xenagos is an indestructible enchantment that becomes a creature when devotion is high. At combat, he gives a creature +X/+X and haste where X is that creature's power — effectively doubling any creature's power. One-shot kills via commander damage or massive trampling creatures.

## Strategy
1. T1-2: Ramp aggressively (mana dorks, Nature's Lore, Rampant Growth)
2. T3-4: Cast Xenagos (5 mana). He's indestructible as an enchantment — very hard to remove.
3. T5+: Drop a big creature, Xenagos doubles its power, swing for 20+. Malignus with Xenagos = exactly lethal to one player.

## Mulligan Guide
- KEEP: 2-3 lands + mana dork/ramp + any big creature (5+ CMC)
- KEEP: Sol Ring + 2 lands + anything
- MULLIGAN: All big creatures and no ramp. No green mana. All ramp and no payoffs.

## Win Conditions
- Malignus + Xenagos = power equal to opponent's life total, doubled. Lethal in one swing.
- Balefire Dragon + Xenagos = 12/12 flying that destroys all opponent creatures on hit
- Pathbreaker Ibex + big board = everything gets +10/+10 and trample
- Aggravated Assault + Savage Ventmaw = infinite combat steps
- Chandra's Ignition on a Xenagos-buffed creature = board wipe + massive damage to all opponents

## Known Weakness
- One threat at a time — removal on the big creature before damage wastes the turn
- No card draw in command zone — relies on Greater Good, Rishkar's Expertise
- Weak to sacrifice effects (Fleshbag Marauder) when you have only one creature
- Xenagos only buffs on YOUR combat — defensive capability is limited
